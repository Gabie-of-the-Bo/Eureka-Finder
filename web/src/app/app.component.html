What is a pandigital operation?

A pandigital operation usually refers to a mathematical expression that uses each digit from 1 to 9 exactly once (or sometimes 0 to 9, depending on context) to form a valid arithmetic equation. The idea is that every digit appears exactly once across the numbers and operations in the expression. Eureka Finder is a simple tool that allows you to find pandigital operations easily using brute force.
